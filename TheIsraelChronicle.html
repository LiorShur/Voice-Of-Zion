Understanding "Palestine"
Historical Origins

The Name "Palestine": A Roman Punishment for Jewish Rebellion

After crushing the Jewish Bar Kokhba revolt in 135 CE, Emperor Hadrian renamed the province of Judea to "Syria Palaestina" — a name derived from the Philistines, the Jews' ancient enemy — specifically to sever the Jewish connection to their homeland. Scholars widely agree this was punitive "de-Judaization" of the land.

Modern Identity

Palestinian Nationalism: A 20th Century Development

Historian Rashid Khalidi's foundational work confirms Palestinian national identity emerged in the early 20th century. Before the British Mandate, Arabs in the region identified primarily as Ottomans, Arabs, or Syrians. The term "Palestinian" applied equally to Jews living there — hence the "Palestine Post" (now Jerusalem Post) and Palestine Symphony Orchestra.

Historical Record

No Independent "Palestinian" State Ever Existed in History

There has never been an independent nation called "Palestine" with its own government, currency, or borders. When Jordan held the West Bank (1948-1967) and Egypt held Gaza, neither established a Palestinian state. The PLO was founded in 1964 — three years before Israel held the West Bank or Gaza.

Frequently Asked Questions

Calm answers to hard questions — because understanding matters more than shouting.

Why do Jews believe they have a claim to this land?
The Jewish people originated in the Land of Israel over 3,000 years ago. Jerusalem was their capital under Kings David and Solomon. They built two Temples there. When exiled by Babylonians and Romans, they never stopped praying to return. Archaeological, historical, and genetic evidence confirms this unbroken connection — the longest documented tie of any people to any land.
Is Israel a "colonial" project imposed by Europeans?
No. Jews are indigenous to Israel, not colonizers from elsewhere. The majority of Israeli Jews descend from refugees expelled from Arab and Muslim countries (Mizrahi Jews), not Europe. Zionism was a movement of return to an ancestral homeland — similar to other indigenous peoples reclaiming their heritage. Unlike colonialism, there was no "mother country" seeking resources.
Why is Israel singled out for criticism more than any other country?
This is the question Israelis and Jews ask constantly. Countries with far worse human rights records — China, Iran, Syria, North Korea — receive a fraction of the UN condemnations Israel does. This obsessive focus on the world's only Jewish state, while ignoring far greater abuses, fits the historical pattern of antisemitism: holding Jews to standards applied to no one else.
Does Israel want peace?
Israel has repeatedly offered land for peace. It withdrew completely from Gaza in 2005, uprooting 8,000 of its own citizens. It offered the Palestinians a state in 2000 (Camp David), 2001 (Taba), and 2008 (Olmert Plan). Each offer was rejected. Israel made peace with every Arab nation willing to make peace. The obstacle has never been Israeli unwillingness.
What is "From the River to the Sea"?
This slogan calls for Palestinian control from the Jordan River to the Mediterranean Sea — the entire territory of Israel. There is no version of this that includes a Jewish state. For Jews who hear it, it is a call for their elimination from their homeland. It is not a peace slogan; it is a rejection of coexistence.
Can I support Palestinian rights and still support Israel's existence?
Absolutely. Many Israelis, Jews, and supporters of Israel believe Palestinians deserve dignity, self-determination, and prosperity. What cannot be accepted is the demand that Jewish self-determination must end for Palestinian self-determination to begin. Two peoples can share this land — but only if both accept the other's right to exist.
Why do Jews feel unsafe when they see anti-Israel protests?
Because history has taught them that anti-Jewish violence follows anti-Jewish rhetoric. When protesters chant for "intifada" (violent uprising), tear down hostage posters, or attack visibly Jewish people, it confirms that the target is not just Israeli policy — it is Jews. Synagogues, Jewish schools, and Jewish businesses have been attacked worldwide during these protests.
If Israel is so strong, why does it need defending?
Military strength protects Israelis from rockets and terrorism. But Israel also faces a war of delegitimization — efforts to deny its right to exist through boycotts, UN resolutions, and campus activism. This "soft war" aims to isolate Israel until it cannot defend itself. History has shown that when Jews cannot defend themselves, catastrophe follows.
Why is Gaza poor if it receives so much aid?
Palestinians have received over $40 billion in international aid since 1994 — among the highest per-capita aid in the world. Hamas diverted billions into military infrastructure: over $1 billion on tunnels alone, plus rockets, weapons factories, and "pay-for-slay" rewards for terrorists. Meanwhile, Hamas leaders became billionaires living in Qatari luxury while their people suffer. Gaza's poverty is a policy choice by its rulers.
Does Hamas really use human shields?
Yes, and they've admitted it publicly. Hamas spokesman Sami Abu Zuhri called on Palestinians to form human shields on Al-Aqsa TV. The UN confirmed rockets in UNRWA schools. The IDF found command centers under hospitals, weapons in mosques, and tunnel entrances in homes. Hamas builds no bomb shelters for civilians — but built 500km of tunnels for fighters. The strategy is explicit: maximize civilian casualties to win global sympathy.
Is Israel's response to October 7th proportionate?
By any historical measure, yes. The US response to 9/11 (3,000 dead) resulted in 20-year wars killing over 400,000 civilians. The Allied response to WWII killed 600,000+ German civilians through bombing alone. Israel's civilian-to-combatant casualty ratio of approximately 1.5:1 is historically low for urban warfare — the UN estimates 90% civilian casualties is typical. West Point's leading urban warfare expert called Israel's efforts to minimize civilian harm "unprecedented in history."
